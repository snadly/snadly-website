## What sits underneath every page

### Speed-to-value is broken

Everyone waits too long before the data is usable. The public copy should express this through each buyer's own world, not through the abstract theme.

### One person is the bottleneck

BI manager: engineer capacity. Analyst: themselves. Owner: nobody technical. Consultancy: expensive senior review time.

### Inaction has a hidden cost

Time, salary, delayed decisions, poor stock bets, low-margin project work, or cloud spend that does not produce insight.

### They know what good looks like

Each persona has seen the destination. The friction is getting there quickly enough and safely enough.

### They have been burned before

Tools that still needed engineers, dashboards without models, fragile spreadsheets, or outputs that could not be owned.

---

## Homepage rule

**The homepage should route, not explain the strategy.**

Do not tell visitors that the product is being marketed through four personas. Let them recognise themselves and click through.

The homepage should carry only the shared promise: connect source systems and get a clean, queryable warehouse in minutes rather than weeks.

---

## Copy guardrails

- **No internal labels.** Avoid "Thread A", "persona", "router", "same engine", or "conversion path" in public pages.
- **No fake proof.** Do not use invented testimonials. Use product examples, sample output, or real proof when available.
- **No overclaims.** Avoid "compliance team stops asking questions" or "best SaaS you pay for". Be specific and defensible.
- **No jargon for owners.** Keep the owner page about decisions and visibility. Keep dbt and star schemas out unless needed in technical pages.
